Rural vs Urban Connectivity C​onnectivity differences between cities and provinces highlight the inequality in digital access.

Urban areas often have faster fiber connections and multiple service providers. Meanwhile, rural areas rely on slower mobile networks. Government programs aim to improve infrastructure, but progress is slow. Private companies also prioritize profitable urban areas. The lack of connectivity impacts not only education but also healthcare services and job applications. Community Wi-Fi hubs and satellite internet are possible solutions for remote areas.

Bridging rural and urban connectivity gaps requires cooperation between government, businesses, and communities.
