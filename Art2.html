W​hen education moved online, not all students were ready. Some had to share one device with their entire family, while others relied only on mobile data.

Online learning requires stable internet for video classes, research, and project submissions. Students without reliable connection often miss deadlines and live sessions. Financial challenges make it difficult for families to buy gadgets. A laptop can be too expensive for households already struggling with daily expenses. In rural communities, poor infrastructure also limits signal strength. Even if students can afford data, the connection may still be weak. This situation affects students’ confidence and mental health because they feel behind compared to their classmates.

Equal access to technology is essential to ensure fair education for all students.
